Palindrome Checker

Palindrome (noun): a word or phrase that reads the same backwards as forwards, e.g. 'Level' or 'Borrow or Rob.'

The SATOR square palindrome found in Pompeii (pre-A.D. 62) is one of the first recorded. Composing Palindromes is thought to have been a pastime of the rural Roman Elite. The words mean:

SATOR
Sower, planter or founder.

AREPO
Though to be someone's name, either invented to complete the palindrome or of non-Latin origin.

TENET
He/She/It holds, keeps, preserves.

OPERA
Service, labor, care.

ROTAS
Wheels.

The square could be interpreted as "Arepo, the sower, holds the wheels carefully" or "The farmer Arepo works his wheels."
